‚úçÔ∏è WRITING YOUR REFLECTION

Your 850‚Äì1000 word reflection must address these four sections:

1. Introduction

Briefly introduce your chosen theme and state the exact titles of the two articles you selected. Provide a clear thesis statement outlining your critical argument or perspective.

2. Summary and Analysis

Succinctly summarise the core argument of each article. What is the author‚Äôs main claim? What evidence do they use? Be precise.

3. Synthesis and Critical Engagement

This is the most important section. Compare and contrast the two articles. Do the authors agree or disagree? How do their arguments complement or challenge each other? Critically evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of their evidence, logic, and assumptions. Connect their findings directly to the South African context and the specific topic under your theme.

4. Conclusion

Summarise your key insights. What does this analysis reveal about your chosen theme? End with a thoughtful closing statement.
